# Dawn Unbroken

The sky hadn't fully brightened yet.

Kixer surfaced toward wakefulness with that recognition—not startled by any sound, just having slept enough, her body naturally receding from the tide of dreams.

*And then, she felt it.*

---

First, her shoulder.

A dull ache, seeping up from deep beneath her skin. People who stay indoors too long always have this affliction—their perception of temperature and touch grows so acute it borders on excessive.

Then, the sound of breathing.

Even, faintly warm, falling just below her collarbone. Once, twice, like some regular tide.

Kixer didn't move.

Her silver-white hair lay scattered in the dimness like unmelted snow, a few strands somehow tangled between her fingers. Under the blanket, one hand rested quietly on her waist—the palm warm, the heat seeping through a thin layer of cotton, like some wordless assertion.

*Garki.*

Kixer hadn't actually thought she would really be lying here.

Last night's events flickered through her mind like a lantern slide: Garki telling her guard she was "staying at a friend's place tonight," her tone as matter-of-fact as if she were reporting the weather; the guard, long accustomed to her running off every few days, asking for the address and withdrawing without question. Then an old set of pajamas tossed onto her head, the sound of running water from the bathroom, and—

"Don't get too close."

"Mmhm."

A sticky, syrupy reply, then inching over bit by bit.

Kixer remembered warning her.

And this was the result.

---

The curtains were Garki's doing—drawn tighter than Kixer's usual habit. Only a narrow gap remained. The morning sea breeze of White Stone City, carrying salt and moisture, slipped through the imperfectly sealed window crack, stirring a hint of coolness through the dim air.

A single line of dawn light bled through that gap, thin and fine, falling across the duvet like a shallow trail of water.

The air conditioner hummed low.

The room was impossibly quiet.

*...So this is what she looks like asleep.*

Kixer turned her head, letting her gaze settle on Garki's face.

The one who was always grinning, eyes curved in mischief—she was strangely peaceful in sleep. Silver-white lashes rested softly. Her lips were parted, just slightly, as though even in dreams she still had words left unfinished.

That thin line of dawn happened to fall across her cheek, tracing her profile in soft, faded outlines.

Kixer didn't know how long she'd been watching.

Then she did something she hadn't fully thought through.

She reached out—so lightly, as if afraid of shattering something fragile—and touched Garki's fingertip with her own.

Just once.

Like confirming something. Like testing something.

---

Garki's lashes stirred.

Kixer's finger hung in midair, not withdrawn.

Garki didn't wake. But the hand resting on Kixer's waist slowly loosened its curled posture—not pulling away, but following that faint touch, the palm settling more snugly against her, natural as water flowing to a lower place.

Then she shifted a little closer to Kixer.

A tiny movement. Her nose brushed against Kixer's neck and stayed there.

*...Troublesome.*

That's what Kixer thought, but she didn't move.

And in that half-awake stillness, the hand that had been obediently resting on her waist—without warning, unhurriedly—slid beneath the hem of her pajamas.

---

Kixer's body stiffened involuntarily the moment she registered the sensation.

*...Garki's fingers are long.*

The absurd thought drifted through her mind. The fingertips carried the morning's coolness, seemingly just seeking warmer places in sleep—sinking into the softness of her inner thigh, rubbing with the lightest, most unconscious motion.

Unconscious.

And precisely because it was unconscious, it was like a dull blade, patiently, repeatedly, wearing down her most vulnerable defenses.

Kixer stared at the ceiling.

Her knuckles were white against the bedsheet.

"...Garki."

Her voice was low, rough with morning.

The breathing pressed against her neck paused.

Garki didn't lift her head, but beneath the blanket, her hand stopped moving. She kept that position, fingers sunk into the soft warmth of Kixer's thigh—and then, through that last thin layer of fabric—

Gently, she pressed down.

Landing precisely on the most secret, most sensitive peak.

"Mn—"

Kixer arched involuntarily, a sticky, muffled moan escaping her throat. She reached to grab Garki's wrist—

Garki was awake.

---

Mint-green eyes slowly focused in the dim light.

No trace of her usual slyness, no triumphant curved smile. Only the solemn, misty seriousness of someone just awakened.

Garki held her position, breath falling against Kixer's neck. Once. Twice. Three times.

"...How long have you been awake."

Her voice was hoarse, rough with sleep, each word squeezed out slowly.

Kixer kept her eyes closed and didn't answer.

Garki laughed.

Kixer could feel it—lips against skin, the upward curve of her smile transmitting through her neck like a small electric current.

"You were pretending to sleep."

Calm, with that unhurried quality unique to mornings.

Kixer finally opened her eyes.

She turned her head and found herself face to face with Garki—nose to nose.

Garki looked down at her own hand, took a serious moment to confirm its position, then lifted her head with an expression of utter honesty.

Beneath the blanket, that hand slowly slid upward, tracing the contour from thigh to waist, bringing with it a faint rustle, finally stopping below her ribs, beside her chest.

Palm pressed flat. Thumb—gently pressed down.

"Heart's beating so fast," Garki said.

Matter-of-fact, as if remarking on today's fine weather.

---

Kixer's ears began to burn.

She averted her gaze, landing on that sliver of light in the curtain, then looked back. Garki was still watching her, lips pursed, the corners curving up.

"...Where's your hand."

"Here," Garki said, with perfect honesty.

*...The words "move it" were right there on her lips.*

Kixer knew it.

Garki knew she knew it.

They held each other's gaze. The golden morning light crept from that crack on the wall, falling across Garki's profile.

Garki pressed her thumb again.

Lightly, through the pajama fabric, warmth seeping in from her palm.

"...It's beating even faster now."

This time her voice dropped lower. Not teasing anymore—more like she was confirming something she herself cared about.

"Garki."

"Mm."

"...Stop looking at me."

Garki blinked.

Then she slowly brought her face closer—nose touching nose. Between their lips, the thickness of a single sheet of paper remained.

"Not looking," Garki said, her lips almost brushing Kixer's. "I closed my eyes."

She really did close them.

Placing all the choice in Kixer's hands—forward or back, advance or retreat, all hinging on a single breath.

Kixer didn't pull away.

She just—her breathing grew softer. Then, slowly, she closed her own eyes.

---

Both with eyes closed.

Noses touching. A sheet of paper between their lips.

Only the low hum of the air conditioner filled the room.

A long time passed.

Maybe a minute. Maybe three.

Garki moved forward by that paper-thin distance, her lips meeting Kixer's.

Lightly, as if touching dawn itself with her mouth.

Then Garki pulled back, just as gently.

She opened her eyes; in those mint-green irises flickered a tenderness close to reverence.

And under cover of that kiss, the hand that had been obediently resting against her ribs slid, unhurriedly, back between her thighs.

When her fingertips truly found that place—

Kixer's breath broke completely.

"Garki... hah..."

It was a gasp utterly unguarded, trembling with sound.

---

Garki's fingers moved slowly.

She wasn't in a hurry to probe deeper. Instead, she used her fingertip to trace the small, subtly twitching softness, circling it with infinite patience, over and over. The cool fingers were soon warmed by Kixer's own heat, becoming just as slick and burning.

"Here," Garki's voice dropped to a near-whisper, her lips pressed to Kixer's flushed ear, "it's warmer than usual, Kixer."

"Shut up... don't—"

Kixer's hand finally caught Garki's wrist.

But her fingers were trembling, without any strength at all, as if she were merely resting them there, urging the other to continue.

Garki's thumb pressed along the seam, pushing inward. Just a shallow pressure, and it drew out a faint, wet, morning sound.

Kixer buried her face deeper, tears spilling at the corners of her eyes.

Garki's fingertips were wicked—following that narrow opening, bit by bit, tentatively, taking in the first knuckle.

"Mmn—! Ga-Garki..."

The feeling of fullness made Kixer instinctively try to close her legs, but Garki's knee nudged them apart with ease.

*...This silence magnified every sensation a hundredfold.*

Kixer could feel, with terrible clarity, Garki's fingertip scraping across the ridges inside, drawing out wave after wave of heat. The finger inside quickened, shifting from monotonous pressure to a rhythm of unhurried thrusts, each withdrawal and entry pulling out clearer, stickier sounds.

Gentle, yet an inexorable plunder.

Then—Garki stopped abruptly.

Her finger still buried deep, in the hottest place, motionless.

"Hah... huh... you—"

Kixer opened her eyes, tears swirling, a mix of confusion and resentment as she glared upward at the other. The emptiness of being suspended mid-peak made her clench instinctively, only to bite deeper around Garki's finger.

Garki looked at her, eyes overflowing with that melt-soft tenderness. She leaned down, pressing her lips to Kixer's again—not in a kiss, just resting there.

"Kixer likes this, doesn't she," Garki murmured against the seam of their lips.

Kixer bit her lower lip hard, nails nearly gouging into Garki's wrist.

The dawn light had finally crept from the curtain gap to the edge of the bed, tracing the tangled shapes beneath the duvet in blurred outline.

So long that Garki thought she would, as usual, answer with silence.

Then Kixer turned her head away, releasing her white-knuckled lower lip.

Her voice was so faint it seemed the wind would carry it off:

"...Don't stop."

---

Garki froze for one second.

Then, those mint-green eyes burst with the brightest light.

The hand beneath the blanket deepened, quickened in an instant—

As Kixer's finally unhinged, tear-choked moan broke free, the morning light in the room blazed fully.

---

Later, the sound of running water came from the bathroom.

Garki leaned against the headboard, looking down at her own long, slender right hand. Her fingertips seemed to still hold the faint, lingering scent of Kixer.

She listened to the water, eyes curved, slowly burying her face in Kixer's pillow. She laughed, softly.

The pillow carried Kixer's scent—light, clean, like all the things she never said aloud, yet undeniably existed.

The water stopped.

Kixer shuffled out slowly, her hair wrapped in a towel. Her ears and neck still bore traces of un-faded flush. She walked toward the bed with a pace much slower than usual.

She didn't look at Garki, going instead to the window and pulling the curtains all the way open.

White Stone City's sunlight flooded the entire room in an instant.

Garki sat in the light, wearing her most harmless smile:

"Kixer—are we not playing games today?"

Kixer walked to her desk, picked up her special cup, and drank a long gulp of cold water. She swallowed, then cast a sidelong glance at her.

"...If your hand's sore, don't talk."

She set down the cup and looked away.

But this time, she didn't walk back to her computer. Instead, she slowly retraced her steps to the bedside—

And at a distance of exactly one fist from Garki, quietly, she sat down.
